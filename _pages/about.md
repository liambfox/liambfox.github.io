# Biography
Liam Fox is a PhD candidate in the Department of Geography & Planning, University of Toronto. His current research examines the cultural politics of energy transition and extractivism in Canada. His dissertation historicizes contemporary extractive populism in Canada, tracing it to previous struggles over jurisdiction dating back to the colonization of what is now called the Prairie West, specifically in the territories of Treaty 6 and 7. He has published work in journals such as _Antipode_ and _Social & Cultural Geography_. He currently lives in Vancouver, the unceded ancestral territories of the Musqueam, Squamish, and Tsleil-Waututh Nations.
